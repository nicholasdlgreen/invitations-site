03

Buttons

3 primary variants · all pill-shaped (border-radius: 60px)
Primary — .btn

Use for the main call-to-action on a section. Never more than one primary button per screen area. Gold on all light backgrounds; dark (Espresso) variant for specific contexts.

Outline — .btn-out

Secondary action — always paired with a primary .btn. Fills to solid gold on hover.

Ghost — .btn-ghost

Tertiary / navigation use only — stepping back through a flow. Border turns gold on hover.

Button Hierarchy — Correct Pairing

A primary action and a secondary action sit side by side. Ghost is used only for backward navigation.

·
On Dark Backgrounds

On Espresso backgrounds: .btn works unchanged. .btn-out should use white border + white text to maintain contrast.

Imagery Guidelines

Tone · subject matter · treatment

Brand Imagery Tone

Photography should feel intimate, light-filled and emotional — as if looking through a window at a private moment, not at a staged shoot. Think: film grain, soft bokeh, warm natural light, minimal props.

✦ Seek Out
  • Soft, golden hour light
  • Negative space & breathing room
  • Paper textures & flat lays
  • Genuine emotional moments
  • Muted, warm colour palettes
  • Hands, details, close-ups
✕ Avoid
  • Oversaturated, HDR edits
  • Posed, stiff compositions
  • Cluttered backgrounds
  • Harsh direct flash/lighting
  • Cold, blue-tinted palettes
  • Generic stock photo feeling
Subject Matter
  • Invitation flat lays
  • Paper & envelope details
  • Couple candid moments
  • Venue establishing shots
  • Hands holding invitations
  • Wax seals, ribbon, detail
Hero Image Specification
Dimensions: 1600px+ width · 16:9 or taller (portrait scenes work well) · min 1MB
Treatment: Overlay gradient applied in CSS — top 80% cream at 80% opacity · mid 62% · bottom 85%. Image must hold its own through this veil.
Content: Should have enough empty sky, table, or architectural space in the upper third for the headline to breathe over.
Source recommendations: Stocksy (editorial feel) · Unsplash (free, curated below) · Commissioned photography when ready.
